Finish Line asset
==================

Structure mirrors your "traffic-cone-3" package:

finish-line-3/
├── textures/                          <- drop-in PBR texture set for your engine
│   ├── M_Finish_Line_BaseColor.png
│   ├── M_Finish_Line_Normal.png
│   ├── M_Finish_Line_Roughness.png
│   ├── M_Finish_Line_Metallic.png
│   ├── M_Finish_Line_Height.png
│   └── M_Finish_Line_OcclusionRoughnessMetallic.png
└── source/
    └── Finish Line/
        ├── finish_line.obj            <- mesh: two poles, sagging checkered
        ├── finish_line.mtl               banner, and a ground decal strip
        └── (same texture set, copied alongside the mesh)

Notes:
- All textures are 2048x2048 to match your cone asset's resolution.
- The mesh is plain OBJ/MTL (not FBX) so it can be imported into any engine
  (Unity, Unreal, Godot, Blender, etc.) without a proprietary importer.
  Import it and re-export/re-save in your engine of choice to get the
  engine-specific .meta/import files, the same way "cone.fbx" got its
  own .meta when it was first imported — those files are generated by
  the importer itself and can't be authored by hand ahead of time.
- Banner and ground strip currently share one texture/material
  (M_Finish_Line). Split the UVs or duplicate the texture if you want the
  ground decal to tile independently.
- Geometry is simple and low-poly on purpose (3 objects, ~350 tris) so it's
  easy to edit further in a DCC tool — sculpt the sag, thicken the poles,
  add a base plate, etc.
